Virtual work is here in a big way with no time to plan or prep. Here is a list of video conferencing tools to get you started. As a first step, we recommend starting with an online chat tool, like Slack.\u00a0 For more tools for small businesses, check this <\/span>Inc article<\/span><\/a>.<\/span><\/p>\n

Online Chat for Business<\/h2>\n

This is the number one tool in our organization. It doesn\u2019t interrupt as a phone call does and you can switch to a phone call with a team member in click as well as pass document links without having to screen share them. If you don\u2019t have this in your organization, get it now.<\/span><\/p>\n

Slack<\/b> \u2013 Your team can see who is online at a glance and call through the computer with a click. Lots of integrations. (Use them!)<\/span><\/p>\n

Hangouts – <\/b>It might make sense if you use G-Suite in your company. With the Meet version, it seems they are working on improving it. We left it for Slack about 1 year ago.<\/span><\/p>\n

Teams – <\/b>Definitely consider if you are on Office 365 or about to adopt it.<\/span><\/p>\n

Do <\/span>this<\/span><\/a> unless you have Office 365 and prefer teams or G-Suite and prefer the advanced Hangouts. (We have G-Suite and use slack).<\/span><\/p>\n

Video and Conference Calling for Business<\/h2>\n

I highly recommend using the video feature in video calls. Your team and customers need face-to-face communication now.<\/span><\/p>\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n\n

Uberconference<\/h3>\n

By far, Uberconference is the easiest to <\/span>sign up<\/span><\/a> with for the most features. No call length limit and 50 users due to Covid-19. We stopped using it a while back as zoom quality seemed better but if you need something fast and simple, try it out.<\/span><\/p>\n

Zoom<\/h3>\n

The only problem is it is difficult to get new accounts due to the volume of demand. Otherwise the free version allows group calls (larger than 3 people) for up to 40 minutes. <\/span>Here<\/span><\/a> is the best guide I have seen on getting started. Can\u2019t sign up? (And this goes for any of the tools) Maybe borrow and account from a friend who is not using it.<\/span><\/p>\n

Microsoft Teams<\/h3>\n

It comes with Office 365, but you can sign up also with a Microsoft or Gmail account. I tested this with my personal outlook.com and it worked though it is important to start with the correct <\/span>link<\/span><\/a> to activate it.\u00a0 (Personal use link…I don\u2019t think they are being too strict about defining personal use right now but something to note if you are a business owner.) The difference between paid and free teams can be found <\/span>here<\/span><\/a> but due to Covid-19 paid teams has a free 6 month trial for now.\u00a0 When I tried to start a conference, it hung so perhaps there is a load issue with so many people getting on.<\/span><\/p>\n

Google Hangouts<\/h3>\n

Hangouts and Duo can work for calls with up to 10 people and are available if you have a Gmail account. However, I do not see a way to set up a dial number with the version linked to a free Gmail account. More advanced features are <\/span>available<\/span><\/a> (And now for no additional charge through July 31, 2020) if you have G-Suite (paid Corporate G-Mail).<\/span><\/p>\n
